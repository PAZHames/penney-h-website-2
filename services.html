About psychotherapy and counselling

In practice there is little difference between psychotherapy and counselling. Both can help with a wide range of concerns, no matter how long you have been struggling with them. What is different is the level of training – training to be a counsellor takes less time than training to be a psychotherapist. I’m a psychotherapist. But I use the term counsellor because it’s the one that most people use.

The perspective we bring to the work is based upon our clinical experience, knowledge of theoretical approaches and experience of our sessions.

What to expect from my counselling service

Counselling is often a term used to describe shorter term therapy, often in response to an event where psychological distress has been experienced over a relatively short period of time.

The sessions involve us meeting, talking and listening together with the mutual purpose of paying careful attention to your concerns and gaining a greater understanding of your own particular situation. It is a shared process and the therapeutic relationship can only be nurtured in a safe and dependable environment.

I work carefully and respectfully, honouring your privacy, autonomy and independence and your freedom and ability to make choices for yourself. Within the therapeutic relationship you can explore your physical and emotional feelings, your thoughts and behaviours and the contexts in which they arise.
